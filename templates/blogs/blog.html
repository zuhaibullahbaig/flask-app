What are Qr codes?

A QR code, short for "Quick Response code," is a two-dimensional barcode that can store various types of information. QR codes were invented in Japan in the 1990s and have since become widespread and popular due to their versatility and ease of use. QR codes consist of black squares arranged on a white background, typically in a square shape, and they can be scanned by a QR code reader or scanner, often found in smartphones and other devices.
